Measuring Urban Accessibility An accurate and scalable approach

Accessibility (to healthcare, green spaces, schools, shopping areas and other points of interest) is an important indicator of both the livability and sustainability of a city.  For this project we have developed an approach that utilizes readily-available public data to quantify urban accessibility.

Our approach measures proximity at the parcel level, is independent of subjectively-defined "access thresholds", and is easily scalable. We apply this tool to multiple US cities to compare accessibility to a range of core urban services. Results can be used by city councils and planning agencies to inform the prioritization of infrastructure developments. For more information, visit the Adapting Cities website.
