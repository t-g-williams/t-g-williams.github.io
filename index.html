Tim G. Williams
PhD

I model resilience in agricultural systems.

My goal as a researcher is to improve understanding of how agricultural systems can transition to become more sustainable (both socially and environmentally) in the face of global change.

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Environmental Geography Group at Vrije Universiteit in Amsterdam. My research here focuses on modelling transformation in European agriculture.

In August 2021, I graduated with a PhD in Industrial & Operations Engineering from the University of Michigan. In my dissertation I used agent-based modelling to examine strategies for building resilience in smallholder agricultural systems. My postdoctoral research builds on this prior experience by transitioning to a European context and a larger scale of modelling.
